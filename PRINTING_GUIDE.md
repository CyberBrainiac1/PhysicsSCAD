# Printing Guide

## Print Assumptions
- Standard FDM printer (approx. 220x220 bed)
- PLA default material
- 0.4 mm nozzle

## Suggested Layer Heights
- 0.2 mm for most boards and manipulatives
- 0.28 mm acceptable for fast classroom batches
- 0.12-0.16 mm for fine label readability

## Wall Thickness Notes
- Minimum 1.2 mm wall (3 lines at 0.4 mm)
- Thin arrows and pegs should keep at least 1.6 mm local thickness for durability

## Support Guidance
- Most models are support-free in default orientation
- Use supports only for optional hanging holes/overhang-heavy variants

## Scaling Notes
- Keep scale near 100% when geometric ratios are part of the lesson
- If scaling for younger students, scale all related parts uniformly

## PLA Assumptions
- Room-temperature classroom use
- Avoid prolonged high-heat storage (e.g., car dashboards)

## Durability Notes
- Thin tabs and labels are demonstrational
- For rough handling, increase thickness parameters by 15-25%

## Printing Classroom Sets
- Batch arrows/tokens in grouped plates
- Use color coding by concept (e.g., forces red/blue, velocity green)
- Keep one "teacher master" with engraved labels and student sets unlabeled
